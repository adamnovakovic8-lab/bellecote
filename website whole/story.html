Our story

Three days became three decades. Here's how a small community picnic grew into the highlight of the Belle Côte summer — and how it stayed true to itself.

How it began

It started with a potluck on the beach

In the summer of 1987, a handful of Belle Côte families set up tables on the shore to thank the fishing crews at the end of a hard season. Word travelled fast along the coast. The next year there were twice as many tables, someone brought a fiddle, and nobody wanted to go home.

That feeling — neighbours and newcomers sharing a meal, a tune and a sunset — is still exactly what the festival is about today.
