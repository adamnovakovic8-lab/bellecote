Everything you need to visit

Belle Côte sits on the Ceilidh Trail along Cape Breton's west coast — an easy, gorgeous drive. Here's how to get here, where to stay, and what to know before you come.

Getting here

Finding Belle Côte

We're about a 1-hour drive from the Canso Causeway and roughly 3 hours from Halifax.

By car

Follow the Ceilidh Trail (Route 19) along the coast. Free festival parking is signed from the village edge.

By air

Fly into Halifax (YHZ) or the seasonal flights to Sydney (YQY), then rent a car for the coastal drive.

Shuttle & park

On Big Saturday, a free shuttle runs from the overflow lot to the main stage every 15 minutes.

Where to stay

Make a weekend of it

From cozy B&Bs to oceanfront campsites, book early — festival week fills up fast.

Cottages & B&Bs

Stay by the shore

Family-run inns and seaside cottages dot the coast within a few minutes' drive.

Camping

Pitch a tent

Nearby provincial and private campgrounds offer ocean views and easy festival access.

Nearby towns

Margaree & Chéticamp

More hotels and restaurants are a short, scenic drive up or down the coast.

Good to know

Frequently asked questions

Absolutely. There are free kids' activities every day — craft tents, the sandcastle showdown, a kids' parade and a lifeguarded family beach day. A handful of evening events are 19+, and those are clearly marked on the schedule.

The festival runs rain or shine. Many events are indoors at the Community Hall, and the main outdoor stage is under a large marquee. Weather calls and any changes are posted to our Instagram and sent to update subscribers.

Leashed, friendly dogs are welcome at the outdoor grounds. For everyone's comfort, pets aren't permitted inside the Community Hall or food tents (service animals always welcome).

The main grounds, hall and washrooms are wheelchair accessible, with accessible parking near the entrance. Some coastal trails are uneven — reach out and we'll help you plan a route that works.
